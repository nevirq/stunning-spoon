Morris Dees

Some highlights:

  • In 1962, Dees represented, Claude Henley, a Ku Klax Klan member who faced federal charges for attacking Freedom Riders. Henley was acquitted, but Dees later said that he regretted his defense of Henley.
  • Founded the Southern Poverty Law Center in 1971
  • Dees was one of the principal architects of an innovative strategy that entailed using civil lawsuits in order to secure a court judgment for monetary damages against an organization for a wrongful act and then using the courts to seize its assets (money, land, buildings, other property) to pay the judgment.
  • In 1981, Dees successfully sued the United Klans of America and won a $7 million judgment for the mother of Michael Donald, a black lynching victim in Alabama. All funds secured in this manner were paid to the fmily of the deceased.
  • Dees's most famous cases have involved landmark damage awards that have driven several prominent neo-Nazi groups into bankruptcy, effectively causing them to disband and re-organize under different names and different leaders.
